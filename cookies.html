Cookies Policy

We use cookies to enhance your experience. This policy explains what cookies are, how we use them, and your choices.

1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, as well as to provide information to the owners of the site. Cookies do not contain personally identifiable information (such as your name or address) unless you have provided that information separately.

Cookies can be "persistent" or "session" cookies. Persistent cookies remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them, while session cookies are deleted automatically when you close your browser.

Our commitment: We use cookies responsibly and only for legitimate purposes. We never sell or misuse cookie data.

2. Types of cookies we use

We use the following categories of cookies on our website:

  • Essential / Strictly Necessary Cookies: These are required for the website to function properly. They enable basic features like page navigation, form submissions, and secure access. You cannot opt out of these cookies as they are essential for the service.
  • Performance and Analytics Cookies: These help us understand how visitors interact with our site by collecting anonymous information (e.g., pages visited, time spent). We use Google Analytics to improve user experience. All data is anonymized.
  • Functionality Cookies: These remember your preferences (e.g., language, region) to provide a more personalized experience. They are non-essential but enhance usability.
  • Marketing / Targeting Cookies: We do not use these for advertising purposes. However, if we ever partner with third-party platforms, we will inform you and obtain explicit consent.

We do not use third-party advertising cookies or cross-site tracking. Your privacy is paramount.

3. How long do cookies last?

Cookies have varying lifespans:

  • Session cookies: Deleted when you close your browser.
  • Persistent cookies: Remain on your device for up to 12 months, unless you delete them manually. We periodically review and renew our cookie settings.

You can clear all cookies from your browser settings at any time. However, disabling essential cookies may affect website functionality.

4. How we use cookies

We use cookies for the following purposes:

  • To provide core functionality: Session management, security, and form processing.
  • To analyze site usage: We collect aggregate data on page views, bounce rates, and traffic sources to optimize content and design.
  • To remember preferences: If you accept cookies, we store your consent status to avoid showing the cookie banner repeatedly.
  • To improve performance: We track loading times and error rates to ensure a smooth experience.

We do not use cookies to collect sensitive personal data or to build user profiles for commercial purposes.

5. Your consent and control

When you first visit our website, we display a cookie consent banner. By clicking "Accept", you consent to our use of non-essential cookies (analytics and functionality). If you click "Decline", we will only use essential cookies.

You can change your cookie preferences at any time by adjusting your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View and delete cookies individually.
  • Block third-party cookies.
  • Disable all cookies (including essential ones).
  • Set preferences for specific websites.

To learn how to manage cookies in your browser, visit the help section of your browser. Please note that blocking all cookies may prevent certain features from working as intended.

6. Third-party cookies

We use a limited number of third-party services that may set cookies:

  • Google Analytics: We use Google Analytics to track site usage. Google Analytics sets cookies that are anonymized and do not identify you personally. You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking by installing the Google Analytics Opt-Out Browser Add-on.
  • Payment processors: When you make a purchase, our payment partners (Stripe, PayPal) may set cookies for fraud prevention and session management. These are governed by their own privacy policies.
  • Social media: We include social media sharing buttons (e.g., Facebook, Instagram) that may set cookies if you interact with them. We do not control these cookies.

We recommend reviewing the privacy policies of these third-party services for more information.

7. Updates to this policy

We may update this Cookies Policy periodically to reflect changes in technology, legal requirements, or our practices. Any updates will be posted on this page with an updated "Last Modified" date. We encourage you to review this policy regularly.

If we make significant changes, we will notify you via a prominent notice on our website or via email (if you have provided it).

8. Frequently asked questions

Can I use the website without accepting cookies?
Yes, but some features may be limited. Essential cookies are still required for basic functionality.

Do cookies contain my personal information?
No. Cookies store non-identifiable data such as session IDs or preference flags. They cannot read your personal files.

How do I delete cookies?
Refer to your browser's settings (usually under "Privacy" or "Clear browsing data"). Each browser has different steps.

Does Growlith use cookies for advertising?
No, we do not use cookies for targeted advertising or retargeting.

9. Legal basis for cookies

Under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the ePrivacy Directive, we are required to obtain your consent for non-essential cookies. Our cookie banner provides clear choices, and we record your consent status for compliance purposes.

You have the right to withdraw consent at any time by changing your browser settings or contacting us. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.
